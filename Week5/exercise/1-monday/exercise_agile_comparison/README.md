# Exercise: Agile vs Waterfall — Case Study Recommendation

**Mode:** Conceptual (analysis)  
**Time:** ~45–60 minutes  
**Relates to:** `written/1-monday/waterfall.md`, `agile-introduction.md`, `agile-vs-waterfall.md`

## Scenario

You are a **delivery advisor** to **MediChart Analytics**, a healthcare startup building **clinical dashboards** for small hospitals.

**Current facts:**

- **Regulators** require an **auditable requirements baseline** and **formal verification** evidence before any hospital can connect **live patient data**.
- The **founding team** expects **weekly learning** from pilot hospitals: workflows, metrics, and report layouts **change often** for the first 9–12 months.
- The team is **8 people** (3 developers, 2 testers, 1 DevOps, 1 designer, 1 product lead). They can sit together daily.
- A **large hospital group** offers a **fixed-price contract** with **frozen scope** for an **18-month** rollout to 40 sites—if MediChart can prove **predictable milestones** and **documentation**.

## Your tasks

1. Read `templates/case_facts.md` (same facts, printable).
2. Write a **1–2 page** recommendation (or structured bullets) that answers:
   - **Which lifecycle** (Waterfall, Agile/Scrum, or **hybrid**) do you recommend for **Phase A** (first 12 months with pilots) and why?
   - How would you handle the **regulatory** documentation demand **without** sacrificing **feedback speed**?
   - What **risks** does your choice create, and what **mitigations** do you propose?
3. Include a **short paragraph** on **testing philosophy**: where does **shift-left** show up in your plan? (`written/1-monday/agile-test-philosophy.md`)

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Clear **choice** per phase (you may split Phase A vs Phase B) with **at least three** justification bullets tied to **concrete** facts from the scenario.
- [ ] **Regulatory** concern addressed explicitly (traceability, evidence, reviews—not hand-waving).
- [ ] **At least two** risks with **mitigations**.
- [ ] **Shift-left** called out with **one** actionable practice (e.g., acceptance criteria in refinement, CI checks).

## Submission

Submit your write-up as PDF/Markdown in your classroom tool. **No code** required.

## Stretch (optional)

Add a **one-sprint** outline: **Sprint Goal**, **three** backlog items (titles only), and **one** **Definition of Done** bullet that includes **testing**.
